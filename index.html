Junjie Luo

Ph.D. candidate @JHU, School of Medicine | On the Job Market

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Health Science Informatics at the School of Medicine, Johns Hopkins University. My research focuses on designing and developing healthcare AI using real-world data, with special emphasis on human factors and behaviors to enhance health and wellness.
My research program centers on large foundation models for healthcare, particularly continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) for diabetes management:

  • Research & Development: I develop novel AI systems tailored to real-world health problems, including large sensor models, agentic AI for patient messaging, and multi-modal sensor data modeling.
  • Implementation & Evaluation: I deploy these models in production environments and evaluate them for both performance and human values such as fairness and robustness.
My research methodology distinctively combines the full AI lifecycle from development to deployment, with strong industry collaborations (WellDoc, DrFirst, MedStar Health). My passion is conducting research that delivers tangible benefits to both patients and healthcare systems.
